The work is fairly simple, after the citizens are naturalized, the volunteers provide them with voter registration forms. They then assist them in filling out the form, which takes 2-3 hours(?). There is a coach who provides explanation and training to the volunteers, which include middle schoolers and high schoolers. SVCU can provide service hours to those who volunteer. Although the work is simple, it is incredibly important, and can help volunteers gain an understanding of how voting and politics work.

Samuel Wang's story: Samuel was forced to come by his mother, just like most of the kids who came ;). However, after the first group of people came and Samuel successfully helped them fill out the form, he happily exclaimed, "I want to vote too. Can I vote?" However, since he is only 11 years old, he has to wait another 7 years. However, his interest in voting was sparked.
